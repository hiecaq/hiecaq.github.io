Acme is a text editor for Plan 9, in the early 1990s. It's written by Rob Pike.

After watching Russ Cox: A Tour of Acme(Cox 2012), my understanding of it is:

  • It is heavily mouse-based, and utilize 3 buttons of mouse to dispatch to different functionalities.
  • Also, all text on it, either in the file displayed or in the UI of windows, are selectable and dispatches to different functionalities depends on the buttons clicked.
  • These functionalities can be defined externally, in mostly any programming languages. For example, |cmd dispatches the chosen region of text into the cmd pipe, and put the output back to replace the text.

Reference

Cox, Russ. 2012. “A Tour of Acme.” September 17, 2012. https://research.swtch.com/acme.
